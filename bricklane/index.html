Designing a better property page

Bricklane enables users to invest in a entire property portfolio but existing research suggested this was not obvious when viewing individual properties on bricklane.com. In addition, the team felt more could be done to show off their properties, which are chosen carefully and lovingly photographed.

My brief was to redesign the way individual properties appear on the Bricklane website to more effectively (and proudly) display each property and communicate how it forms part of the product.

Tom, the design head and Bricklane co-founder (who provided direction throughout the project) gave me an initial starting point — he imagined something similar to a 'playlist' where users viewing any individual property would understand it was just one of many other properties within a easily navigable collection.

The existing property page design

I began digging through various well-known listings sites to get a better sense of the conventions used for displaying properties. I also looked at other investment platforms to better understand the design language used for stocks, shares and funds.

An example page from robinhood.com

I spotted the opportunity for immediate improvement by moving the property page contents into the same layout as other pages on the marketing site, so that any visitors would actually see the Bricklane brand and easily navigate to other site sections. This change would naturally result in including the stylish typography & components consistent with other landing pages; it would give space to show off the beautiful photography; and it would provide more room to spell out the unique value of Bricklane.

The ‘fund’ component evolved during the project as a way of clearly explaining that Bricklane offers investments in city portfolios rather than individual properties whilst also illustrating the impressive volume of superior properties that Bricklane owns. As part of the wider story told by the new page design, this component (which called back to the original 'playlist' idea) also provided a natural opportunity to invite users to begin the signup process.
